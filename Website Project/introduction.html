about us

TO KNOW MORE

There is an overwhelming demand to understand student and teacher experience during the period of recovery from the pandemic. Based on an OECD (2021) report, “Students’ social and emotional skills are strong predictors of school grades across students’ background, age cohorts, and cities. Additionally, evidence also suggests that the relationship between social and emotional skills and school performance is nuanced- some skills are essentially uncorrelated with school performance but other skills, most notably persistence and curiosity, are strongly related to higher school performance for both 10- and 15-year-olds.” There is a concrete need to help our learners and teachers to develop crucial life skills anchored on the following

A. Life Learnings
B. Growth Mindset
C. Socio-emotional Learning principles
D. Tenets of the Whole Child
E. PERMA Model of Wellbeing

Moreover, an attempt to respond to the concrete need is the development of curated materials and activities in the form of a digital playbook. In sports, a playbook contains descriptions of all the plays and strategies used by a team, often accompanied by diagrams, issued to players for them to study and memorize before the season begins. As a terminal requirement in GED 107, students enrolled in this course would have to develop a digital playbook that will help learners, teachers, parents, and other stakeholders in the attainment of learners maximum social-emotional potential.

course descriptions

COURSE PROFILE


A. 3 unit course
B. Self-Paced Course
C. Covers two fold concepts namely: Psychological perspective and Social perspective of Education.
D. Acknowledges individual recognition of the obligation to self and others for their disciplines and actions and awareness of the contributions of other disciplines to topics, issues, and questions of interest through the involvement of graduate students in communities of practice as they formulate theoretical framework appropriate to their work in collaborative partnerships with faculty and other students of diverse backgrounds.
E. Addresses and solves the emerging psycho-social issues in the Philippine setting.
F. Help teachers to be more socially, efficient, responsive to the needs of the students of the country and the world.

COURSE ADMINISTRATOR



1. Coordinates with master class teachers, assessors, learners and academic supervisors.
2. Help learners get on board the course.
3. Attend to the details of learner access to google classroom and LinkedIn Learning (for specific course only)
4. Manage course information to learners and their queries in a timely manner
5. Perform recording, monitoring and analysis of learner attendance and engagement data.

COURSE OUTCOMES


The course will produce learners who are able to:

1. Promote opportunities for Purposeful Learning to thrive in their classrooms.
2. Support the whole child framework to mirror an inclusive classroom
3. Understand the importance of Social Emotional Learning as a key component of Purposeful Learning.
4. Reflect on one's well-being routines as an educator and redirect should be a need.
5. Infuse their teaching and learning practices with Grit, Resilience and Growth Mindset.
